Four days that started in the city and ended up out on the water. Davao
to ease in, Samal for the heart of it, and a long drive home to bookend
the whole thing.

## Day one — landing in Davao

The trip opened on food, the way Davao trips should. Conveyor-belt sushi
at Sushi Station in SM City Davao — plates gliding past, no menu to
agonize over — then a slow afternoon at Cake Gallerie, the Ghibli-themed
cafe where Totoro keeps watch from the shelves and the heat falls away
at the door. We checked into an Airbnb at Seawind in Sasa, on the quiet
north edge of the city, deliberately staged close to the wharf for an
early start.

## Day two — out to the islands

First light at Sta. Ana Wharf: brine and diesel, outriggers loading up,
the gulf opening flat and bright past the breakwater. We met the joiner
group and pushed off for **Talicud** — water so clear the boat seemed to
hover over the reef, a string of coves to swim and snorkel through, and
a simple grilled lunch with sand still on our feet. Back on the mainland
we refueled on wings at Buffalo's Wings N' Things in SM Lanang, then made
a late, dark check-in at Cavenico il Mare on Samal — the resort holding
its reveal for the morning.

## Day three — Cavenico, then back across

Daylight finally showed off the cove, and we worked through the full
menu of water toys: the banana boat whipping behind the speedboat, kayak
loops, stand-up paddle on the glassier stretches, easy swims in between.
By midday we'd crossed back to meet Ma'am Laiza at SM City Davao, then
settled into One Oasis in Ecoland — a poolside condo cluster — for the
final night.

## Day four — the road home

Checked out the next morning and pointed the car south, the trip winding
down on the long drive back to General Santos.
