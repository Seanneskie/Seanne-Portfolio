Some trips are about the destination; this one was about the city
itself. Three easy days in Davao with no grand itinerary — just a home
base, good food, and time to wander between them.

## Base camp

We set up at a BnB in NorthPoint by Camella, a quiet residential
subdivision tucked away from the traffic — tidy streets and the hush of
a neighborhood, the kind of place you're glad to come back to each
evening. Bags down, the rest of the trip arranged itself around meals
and short hops across the city.

## Eating through the city

Davao runs on its food, and we leaned in. Coffee and an hour of focus at
the Starbucks inside SM Lanang Premier on J.P. Laurel Avenue. A heavier
detour to Zark's Burgers at SM City Davao for one of those teetering,
oversized stacks. And the standout: unlimited fried chicken at Wingboss
on Mamay Road in Buhangin — baskets of it, hot and crackling, refills
landing until you finally wave the kitchen off.

## A break above the mall

The one proper "activity" was an afternoon at Altitude, the rooftop
entertainment zone crowning SM Lanang — arcade lights and claw games
inside, open-air rides out on the sky park, and a breeze that kept the
Davao heat from ever feeling like work. Tickets counted, traded in, and
that was the trip: short, well-fed, and easy.
